Land Registry Digital Service

The Land Registry are working with The Government Digital Service (GDS) to build a new digital service. The service will form a part of GOV.UK and is currently in alpha phase following guidance in the Government Service Design Manual in order to meet the Government Digital By Default Service Standard.

The service is being built in the open, with the code developed as open source, and the architecture and other documentation published under the open government licence.

The alpha will be a minimal viable product of :

  • The irreducible core of a digital first Land Registry, from the user interface to the system of record, a single digital service that meets the needs of conveyancers, proprietors, lenders and caseworkers while meeting statutory requirements.
  • This means the ability to create, store, find, apply to change and change register entries, while showing how fraud can be designed out and security designed in
  • It will not have every feature, only the minimum required to operate the service end-to-end, but it will be a useable system that we can test with real users and quickly turn into beta. The ‘spine’ of a system to build upon.

This will continue to build a digital culture within Land Registry through the adoption of Agile and Multidisciplinary Teams
